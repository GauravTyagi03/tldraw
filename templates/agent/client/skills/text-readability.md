- Keep labels short. Split long ideas into multiple shapes or a separate text annotation.
- Ensure text has enough width to wrap cleanly and does not overlap nearby shapes.
- Use consistent text size for peer items and larger text only for headings or primary concepts.
- Prefer direct nouns and verbs in labels; avoid sentence-length labels inside small shapes.
- After creating or editing text, review for clipping, awkward wrapping, and overlap.
