- Use arrows for meaningful relationships, not decoration.
- Connect arrows to the relevant shapes whenever possible.
- Make arrows visually readable: prefer longer, clearly routed arrows over short cramped connectors.
- Keep arrow paths simple and never route arrows through text labels.
- Avoid arrow-to-arrow intersections. Crossed or haphazard arrows make the whole diagram harder to understand.
- When several arrows are needed, space shapes apart enough that each arrow has its own clear path.
- Use direction consistently, usually left-to-right or top-to-bottom.
- If a flow branches, make branch points explicit with spacing, labels, or a decision shape.
